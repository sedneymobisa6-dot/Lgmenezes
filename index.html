The Founder

Luis G. Menezes

Luis G. Menezes is the architect of one of Kenya's most enduring legal institutions. A graduate of the Kenya School of Law (1969), he was among the pioneering generation of Kenyan advocates who built the country's legal infrastructure in the post-independence era.

Before founding the firm, Mr. Menezes served as a lecturer at the University of Nairobi — a formative experience that shaped his conviction that the law must be accessible, understood and applied with rigour. On 11th May 1970, he opened L.G. Menezes & Co Advocates on Miriu Street in Kisumu, beginning a practice that would grow to define legal service across Kenya.

More than five decades later, the firm he founded continues to carry forward his founding principles: depth of knowledge, personal service and an unwavering commitment to the client's best interest.

Our Guiding Principles

Grounded in the Constitution of Kenya

Our practice is expressly guided by the National Values and Principles of Governance set out in Article 10 of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010. These values are binding constitutional obligations that govern every act of legal interpretation, legislation and public policy in the Republic.

📜

Article 10(1), Constitution of Kenya 2010 — "The national values and principles of governance in this Article bind all State organs, State officers, public officers and all persons whenever any of them applies or interprets the Constitution, enacts, applies or interprets any legislation, or makes or implements public policy decisions."

🇰🇪

Patriotism & National Unity

Article 10(2)(a) demands patriotism, national unity and the sharing and devolution of power. Our counsel consistently advances the constitutional architecture of a united, indivisible Republic.

🏛️

Rule of Law

The rule of law under Article 10(2)(a) is the supreme standard. Every argument we advance and every document we draft is tested against this constitutional imperative.

👥

Democracy & Public Participation

Article 10(2)(a) guarantees democracy and participation of the people. We equip clients to meaningfully exercise these rights in constitutional petitions and public participation processes.

⚖️

Human Dignity, Equity & Social Justice

Article 10(2)(b) enshrines human dignity, equity, social justice, inclusiveness, equality and non-discrimination. These principles shape how we frame every client case.

🔍

Transparency & Accountability

Article 10(2)(c) binds all persons to transparency and accountability in the exercise of power. We hold ourselves to this standard in our billing, advice and conduct before the courts.

🌱

Sustainable Development

Article 10(2)(d) recognises sustainable development as a national value. In advising on commercial and land matters, we are mindful of the intergenerational obligations the Constitution places on all.
